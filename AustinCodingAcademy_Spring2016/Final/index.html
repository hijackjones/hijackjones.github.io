What does Domain Scrubber do?

Domain Scrubber is brought to you by the fine folks at Scrubbly.com. It's been a long time in the works, and finally, after I had so many nice folks ask about domain suppression I decided to build an app that will do just that.

Domain Scrubber is a lightweight desktop app that will allow you to scrub domains from your mailing lists. It's as simple as typing in the domain(s) you'd like to remove from your amiling list, and Domain Scrubber will automatically remove all email addresses that are associated with the domains that you specify. Oh, and it's blindingly fast!

How does it work?


Browse for your mailing list

Your mailing list can be a multi-column CSV file with all email addresses in the first column. Simply browse for your mailing list and import it into Domain Scrubber. Note that your mailing list can also be a simple single column text file.


Enter your domain(s)

Enter any domains that you would like to scrub from your mailing list. For example, if you want to suppress anyone with an @aol.com email address, simply enter 'aol.com.' You can scrub multiple domains by entering each domain on its own line.

Frequently Asked Questions


Does Domain Scrubber work on Mac and PC?

Absolutely. Domain Scrubber is available for both OS X and Windows. Both versions of the application were built in their respective frameworks, but work and perform identically.

I still see domains that I wanted scrubbed out.

Please ensure that your mailing list is properly formatted. The email address should be in the very first column of your CSV file. There should also be no leading or trailing spaces in the email address.

Can I scrub out MD5 hashed domains?

Unfortunately not. We promote the sharing of plain text domain suppression lists. For the time being, hashed domain suppression is not a supported feature of Domain Scrubber.
